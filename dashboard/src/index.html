Research Framing

Bird rights and the supermax create a structural incentive for stars to stay with their current team, while the fresh-start effect suggests performance can improve after a move. In short, the money says stay and the psychology says move.

The core question is whether players who re-sign on supermax deals underperform contract-equivalent peers who change teams.

We measure this with three treatment groups, re-signed supermax, re-signed standard, and changed teams, using EPM outcomes from 2016-17 through 2024-25.

Read the full research framing

The Question

Do supermax re-signings trail otherwise comparable players who moved teams after controlling for age and pre-signing trajectory?

The Data

Nine NBA seasons, 2016-17 to 2024-25, with contract classification and EPM-based performance outcomes.

The Method

Compare outcomes across the three treatment groups to isolate movement versus stay effects under contract context.
